# HR Employee Attrition Dashboard (Excel)

An interactive Excel dashboard that analyzes employee attrition patterns across departments, job roles, education fields, gender, marital status, and age groups — built using PivotTables, PivotCharts, and Slicers.

## 📊 Overview

This dashboard provides HR teams with a quick, visual snapshot of workforce attrition to help identify which employee segments are most at risk of leaving, so retention efforts can be targeted more effectively.

## 🔑 Key Metrics (KPI Cards)

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| **Total Employees** | Total headcount in the dataset |
| **Total Attrition** | Number of employees who left |
| **Attrition Rate** | Total Attrition ÷ Total Employees |
| **Active Employees** | Current employees still with the company |
| **Average Age** | Average age of employees |
| **Average Monthly Income** | Average monthly salary across employees |

## 📈 Visualizations

- **Attrition by Department** – Column chart comparing attrition across Human Resources, Research & Development, and Sales
- **Attrition by Gender** – Donut chart showing male vs. female attrition split
- **Attrition by Job Role** – Bar chart breaking down attrition by role (Sales Rep, Research Scientist, Manager, etc.)
- **Attrition by Education Field** – Pie chart of attrition by field of study (Life Sciences, Medical, Marketing, etc.)
- **Attrition by Marital Status** – Column chart comparing Divorced, Married, and Single employees
- **Attrition by Age Group** – Column chart showing attrition distribution across age ranges (18–60)

## 🎛️ Interactive Filters (Slicers)

The dashboard includes slicers for dynamic filtering by:
- Age
- Job Role
- Gender
- Department
- Education Field
- Marital Status

Selecting any slicer value automatically updates all charts and KPI cards.

## 🚀 How to Use

1. Download `HR_Attrition_Dashboard.xlsx`
2. Open in Microsoft Excel (Enable Editing/Content if prompted)
3. Use the slicers on the left/right panels to filter by Age, Department, Gender, Job Role, Education Field, or Marital Status
4. All charts and KPI cards will update automatically based on your selections

## 📌 Insights You Can Explore

- Which department has the highest attrition?
- Is attrition higher among a specific gender or age group?
- Are certain job roles or education fields more prone to turnover?
- How does marital status correlate with attrition?
